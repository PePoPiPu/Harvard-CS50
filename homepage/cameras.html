About my cameras:

I own a Nikon Coolpix p900 and a Yashica medium format camera from the 70s.

A sample image of a Nikon Coolpix P900

The P900 is a bridge camera with a 35mm-2000mm range and 16MP. It's pretty versatile and operates fairly well at full light conditions. Its low light operability is pretty poor, it having an ISO range of 100-6400. It can reach and ISO equivalent of 12800 when used in High ISO monochrome mode.

Images taken with the Nikon Coolpix P900

Great example of the P900 zoom capabilities. This was taken a good 75 meters away from a deer pack.

My very own Yashica-Mat.

This picture was actually taken with the Nikon P900.

The Yashica-Mat has been an good overall introduction to film photography and medium format photography. It's a shame that the cheapest digital medium format camera is 3000$. The only downside to this camera is that the selenium light meter is broken and taking light measurements can be annoying. Since I don't own a meter I meter with my digital camera and calculate exposure times according to the 120mm film light sensitivity.

Yashica-Mat's viewfinder

Taking pictures with the Yashica can be pretty enjoyable since you have a very close idea of how the final picture is going to look like thanks to its viewfinder.

It operates through the use of mirrors. The view you get is the view of the upper lens and the picture you take is the bottom lens. I wonder if the viewfinder mirror actually compesates for the height difference.

My favorite picture I have taken with the Yashica.

The high contrast, circular lens distortion, fine film grain and the lens shallow focal point are all things I enjoy about the Yashica-Mat.
